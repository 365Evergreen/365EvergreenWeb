Power Automate Best Practices for Reliable Cloud Flows

June 20, 2026 · 9 min read

Think like a solution, not a single flow

It’s tempting to build one giant flow that does everything. In practice, this leads to brittle, hard-to-maintain automation. Instead, think in terms of modular solutions: multiple flows, shared connections, and clear responsibilities.

Use solutions and environments

For anything beyond a quick personal automation, build flows inside solutions. Solutions enable ALM: you can move flows between dev, test, and production environments, use environment variables, and manage dependencies cleanly.

Design for failure and retries

  • Configure retry policies: For actions that call external services, use custom retry policies where appropriate.
  • Use scopes with run-after: Group actions into scopes and configure run after settings to handle Failed, Timed out, or Skipped states gracefully.
  • Log errors: Write error details to a central log (SharePoint list, Dataverse table, or Application Insights via HTTP) for later analysis.

Keep flows readable

Flows are often maintained by people who didn’t build them. Use clear naming and structure:

  • Rename every action to something meaningful, not “Compose 17”.
  • Add comments using notes to explain non-obvious expressions.
  • Use child flows to encapsulate reusable logic.

Manage connections and permissions

Connections are security boundaries. Avoid building production flows that run under a personal account. Use service accounts where allowed by policy, and ensure DLP policies are configured to prevent risky data movement.

Monitor and optimize

Once a flow is live, your job isn’t done. Use the Power Automate analytics and run history to monitor failures, performance, and usage. Identify long-running actions, unnecessary loops, and noisy error patterns, then iterate on the design.
