Building Your First Approval Flow in Power Automate

June 20, 2026 · 7 min read

Why approvals are a perfect starter scenario

Approval workflows are one of the most common use cases for Power Automate. They’re easy to understand, highly visible to stakeholders, and deliver immediate value by reducing email ping-pong and “who owns this?” confusion.

Designing the approval process

Before you open Power Automate, define the basics:

  • Trigger: What event starts the approval? (e.g., new SharePoint item)
  • Approvers: Single approver, multiple approvers, or a group?
  • Outcome logic: What happens on approve vs. reject?
  • Notifications: Who needs to be informed and how?

Creating the flow

  1. Create a new automated cloud flow. Choose a trigger such as “When an item is created” for your SharePoint list or “When a new response is submitted” for Microsoft Forms.
  2. Add an approval action. Use the Start and wait for an approval action. Configure the approval type (e.g., “Approve/Reject – First to respond” or “Everyone must approve”) and set the approvers dynamically from the item or statically from a list of users.
  3. Branch on the outcome. Add a condition that checks the Outcome property of the approval action. In the If yes branch, perform your “approved” logic; in the If no branch, handle rejections.
  4. Send notifications. Use Outlook or Teams connectors to notify the requester and any stakeholders of the decision, including comments from the approver.

Persisting approval data

Don’t leave approval decisions buried in emails. Store the outcome, approver, date, and comments back into your data source (e.g., SharePoint columns or Dataverse columns). This makes reporting and auditing possible later.

Tips for production-ready approvals

  • Use environment variables for approver addresses in managed solutions.
  • Handle timeouts by defining what happens if nobody responds.
  • Localize content if your organization operates in multiple languages.
  • Test with real users to validate the approval experience on mobile and Teams.
