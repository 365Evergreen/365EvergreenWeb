# 🧱 Contact Section Component – User Stories

***

## 🔷 1. Core contact story

### User story

**As a potential client not yet ready to commit**,  
I want a low-friction way to get in touch,  
so that I can ask questions or explore further.

### Acceptance criteria

* Section is clearly identifiable as contact
* Positioned before final CTA or as alternative path
* Does not feel like a heavy form or commitment
* Encourages conversation, not submission

***

## 🔷 2. Contact method clarity story

### User story

**As a user seeking contact details**,  
I want clear options for reaching out,  
so that I can choose what suits me.

### Acceptance criteria

* At least one clear method:
  * email
  * form
  * link to booking
* Options are easy to understand
* No unnecessary fields or complexity

***

## 🔷 3. Tone and messaging story

### User story

**As a cautious visitor**,  
I want the contact section to feel approachable and professional,  
so that I am comfortable reaching out.

### Acceptance criteria

* Language is conversational:
  * e.g. “Have a question?” or “Get in touch”
* Avoids corporate or formal language
* No pressure or urgency
* Aligns with CTA tone

***

## 🔷 4. Minimal form story (if used)

### User story

**As a user filling out a form**,  
I want the process to be quick and easy,  
so that I am not discouraged from completing it.

### Acceptance criteria

* Form includes minimal fields:
  * name
  * email
  * message
* Optional fields clearly marked
* No long or complex forms
* Submission feedback is clear (success state)

***

## 🔷 5. Trust and reassurance story

### User story

**As a user sharing personal details**,  
I want reassurance about how my information is handled,  
so that I feel safe submitting the form.

### Acceptance criteria

* Subtle trust messaging (e.g. “No spam, just a reply”)
* No aggressive data capture language
* Clear and respectful tone

***

## 🔷 6. Interaction story

### User story

**As a user interacting with the contact section**,  
I want a smooth and predictable experience,  
so that I can engage without confusion.

### Acceptance criteria

* Form inputs have clear focus styles
* Button provides hover/active feedback
* Errors are clearly communicated (if validation applied)
* Submission action is immediate and visible

***

## 🔷 7. Visual hierarchy story

### User story

**As a scanning visitor**,  
I want the contact section to be easy to understand at a glance,  
so that I can decide quickly whether to engage.

### Acceptance criteria

* Clear heading and supporting text
* Form or contact details organised cleanly
* Good spacing between elements
* No overcrowding

***

## 🔷 8. Mobile usability story

### User story

**As a mobile user**,  
I want to complete contact actions easily,  
so that I can engage on any device.

### Acceptance criteria

* Inputs are full-width and easy to tap
* Button is large enough for touch interaction
* Layout stacks cleanly
* No horizontal scrolling

***

## 🔷 9. Relationship to CTA story (important)

### User story

**As a visitor evaluating options**,  
I want flexibility in how I engage,  
so that I can choose a comfortable next step.

### Acceptance criteria

* Contact section complements (does not replace) main CTA
* CTA remains primary conversion path
* Contact acts as a softer alternative path
* Visual hierarchy reflects this relationship
