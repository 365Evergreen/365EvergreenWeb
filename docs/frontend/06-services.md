# 🧱 Services Component – User Stories

***

## 🔷 1. Core Section Story

### User story

**As a potential client**,  
I want to understand how you can help me,  
so that I can see if your offering aligns with my needs.

### Acceptance criteria

* The heading clearly communicates capability (e.g. “We make Microsoft 365 work properly”)
* Services are easy to scan
* No unnecessary technical depth at this stage
* Each service feels distinct and valuable
* The section connects naturally to the outcome described earlier

***

## 🔷 2. Service Card – Information architecture

### User story

**As a client dealing with messy content**,  
I want to understand how structure can improve clarity,  
so that I feel confident my information can be organised.

### Acceptance criteria

* The title is clear and non-technical
* Supporting text emphasises clarity and simplicity
* The benefit is immediately obvious
* No jargon (taxonomy, metadata, etc.)

***

## 🔷 3. Service Card – Process automation

### User story

**As someone experiencing inefficiency**,  
I want to see how repetitive tasks can be reduced,  
so that I understand the time-saving potential.

### Acceptance criteria

* The message focuses on reducing manual effort
* The wording is outcome-focused, not tool-focused
* The card aligns directly with the earlier “manual processes” problem

***

## 🔷 4. Service Card – Modern intranet

### User story

**As a user disengaged with current systems**,  
I want to see how a central, usable platform could improve my experience,  
so that I feel this will actually be adopted.

### Acceptance criteria

* The benefit emphasises usability and engagement
* Avoids describing features or technology
* Focuses on “a place people actually use”

***

## 🔷 5. Service Card – Governance

### User story

**As a decision-maker**,  
I want to feel confident that systems can be controlled and maintained,  
so that I trust the long-term sustainability.

### Acceptance criteria

* The message conveys control without rigidity
* The tone avoids fear (no compliance-heavy language)
* Focus on clarity, ownership, and confidence

***

## 🔷 6. Interaction story

### User story

**As a user exploring services**,  
I want subtle visual feedback on interaction,  
so that the experience feels modern and responsive.

### Acceptance criteria

* Cards have hover (lift + shadow)
* Transitions are smooth and fast
* Cards are clickable or clearly interactive
* No heavy animations

***

## 🔷 7. Content tone story

### User story

**As a potential client**,  
I want the services to feel credible and well-defined,  
so that I trust the expertise behind them.

### Acceptance criteria

* Tone is professional and understated
* No buzzwords or exaggerated claims
* Language aligns with consulting (not SaaS marketing)

***
